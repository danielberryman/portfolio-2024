Vision

My goal: Apply my ever-growing technical knowledge to a company's unique value proposition.

Vision can make or break you. The tech field moves at lightning speed. When I read my goal statement above, it sometimes feels simplistic.

It's not.

1. If you don't understand the unique value proposition of the company you work for, then you don't understand the problem. To operate at a high level, you need a deep understanding of the game you're trying to win. Understanding the actual problem is half the battle.

2. The second half of the battle is more obvious. Once you know the problem you want to solve, you need the technical prowess to solve it. But the "problem" with problems is that most of them have tons of possible solutions. You need to understand what tools are available, how to use them, and in which situations to apply them.

These two concepts guide my work. What does that really look like? If you're interested in more details, check out my page on process.
