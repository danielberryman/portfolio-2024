Process

A process delivers exactly what it’s designed to deliver. A great process guarantees outstanding solutions and consistently delivers them.

Does yours?

How do you create that kind of process for yourself & your team?

Here's my approach: Own the process. Prepare like crazy. Measure success. Iterate.

Owning The Process: We've all heard the saying that we're our own worst enemy. To me, 'owning the process' means acknowledging that we, to a huge degree, are our own biggest obstacle. In a way, we're the problem! That sounds bad. I don't mean it like that. Acknowledging this invites us to admit what we don't know, ask question to learn, measure our performance, and keep iterating.

Prepare Like Crazy: Once you identify the "big" problem, it breaks down into smaller and smaller problems. This isn't a new concept in science and tech but it’s fundamentally true. Then you prepare for each individual problem by asking as many questions as possible to define the problem in detail. There's nothing more important than this. Every software cycle should focus on refining this ability, for both individuals and teams, and, over time, further questioning should become rare. In fact, that's a great thing to measure.

Measure success: Looking at ourselves in the mirror is tough. I’ve noticed that people tend to avoid this for a variety of reasons. Some are afraid they’ll fall short and face criticism, while others worry it might raise the bar for company expectations. But if we want to "guarantee outstanding solutions and consistently deliver them," we can't avoid measuring our success. The good news is we're rewarded with countless benefits! We gain clarity about where we stand and what’s expected of us. As we grow, we build confidence and momentum. Ultimately, we get to deliver high-quality, high-impact work with a sense of joy.

Iterate: Even after doing all of the above, nothing's perfect. As we produce high-quality, high-impact work, mistakes will happen. Fix them, improve, and move forward. There’s no way around it. Letting go of perfectionism is what keeps the momentum going and the work joyful.
